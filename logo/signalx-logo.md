# The SignalX mark

The SignalX logo fuses two letterforms — **Σ** and **X** — into a single shape. The two halves carry the two halves of the framework's identity: a reactive runtime, expressed in TSX.

## Σ — the signal

The left half is shaped after the Greek capital letter **Sigma**. In mathematics, Σ is the symbol of *summation* — the operator that gathers many values into one result. In SignalX, that is exactly what reactivity does: many fine‑grained signals are summed, every render, into the view a user sees.

Σ also reads as **Sig**, the first syllable of *Signal* and of the package name `sigx`. It carries the formal, mathematical lineage that the reactivity model owes to fine‑grained reactive primitives.

## X — the TSX

The right half is a clean geometric **X**. It stands for **TSX** — the authoring surface of every SignalX component — and for the way reactive flows cross and converge inside the runtime: from signal to computed, from computed to effect, from effect to DOM.

The X is also the part of the mark that gives the logo its modern, technical character. It marks SignalX as a tool for the JSX‑native generation of frameworks rather than a template‑first one.

## Σ + X = SignalX

Read together, the two shapes spell the name. State (Σ) transforms into views (X). The mark is the framework, in two strokes.
